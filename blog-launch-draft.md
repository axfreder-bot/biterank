## We Rank Food, Not Restaurants

**TL;DR:** BiteRank is a restaurant discovery app that thinks about food the way you do — dish by dish, craving by craving. Swipe through the best tacos al pastor in Austin, the finest tonkotsu ramen in Seattle, the ultimate bagel in NYC. Food Swiper is the game. Dish search is the utility.

---

### The Problem

When you're craving ramen, you don't want "a Japanese restaurant." You want the *best* ramen. Maybe you want spicy tonkotsu with the richest broth. Maybe you want delicate shio. Maybe you want vegetarian.

Yelp gives you restaurants sorted by review count and recency. Google Maps gives you proximity. Neither gives you what you actually want: *the best [dish] near me*.

That's the gap BiteRank fills.

---

### How It Works

**Dish Search (Utility)**
Search for any dish — "ramen," "fish and chips," "tacos al pastor" — and get ranked results based on what real people actually ordered and reviewed. Not the closest. Not the most popular. The best for that specific dish.

Each dish page shows:
- Community rankings (what worked for others)
- Specific dishes with ratings
- Photos, notes, "Pioneer" badges for first reviewers
- Links to order via DoorDash

**Food Swiper (Discovery)**
Not sure what you want? Food Swiper shows you restaurants near you, two at a time. Swipe right on what you'd eat right now. Get matches. Build lists. Earn XP.

Think of it as Tinder for people who take their food seriously.

---

### The "We Rank Food, Not Ambiance" Angle

BiteRank is built for people who care more about what's on the plate than what's on the walls. No star ratings for "service" or "decor." Just one question: **was this dish worth coming back for?**

This is food-first discovery. The restaurant is the container. The dish is the star.

---

### The Game Layer

Every review, every swipe, every match earns XP. Level up from Foodie (Lv. 1) to Critic (Lv. 10+). Earn badges for being first to review ("🚩 Pioneer"), for consistency ("🔥 7-day streak"), for covering a cuisine completely ("Taco Tracker").

The gamification isn't superficial — it's the retention mechanism. Duolingo proved that streaks and XP make learning a habit. BiteRank proves that food discovery can work the same way.
